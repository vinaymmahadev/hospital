What is Appendicitis?

Appendicitis is the inflammation of the appendix, often causing sharp pain in the lower right abdomen. It requires urgent treatment, typically through surgical removal to prevent rupture and complications.

Frequently Asked Questions

What causes appendicitis?

Appendicitis is often caused by a blockage in the appendix, commonly due to hardened stool, infections, or inflammation. In some cases, a foreign body or even intestinal worms can block the opening. A poor diet lacking in fiber and high in processed foods can increase the risk. Lack of movement or prolonged sitting may also contribute over time. Maintaining a healthy lifestyle and staying hydrated can help reduce the chances.

Is appendicitis dangerous?

Yes, appendicitis can be dangerous if left untreated. It may lead to rupture, causing a serious infection called peritonitis. Timely diagnosis and surgical removal of the appendix usually prevent complications. Mild symptoms can escalate quickly, so prompt medical care is essential. Early treatment leads to faster recovery and fewer risks.
